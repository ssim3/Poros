
##  **Resistors**

- Made of carbon
- Limits current flow in circuits

Resistors are used to control voltage and current in circuits. They are measured in Ohms ($\ohm$) (See [[Ohm's Law]])

#### 4 Colored Band Resistors:

![[Pasted image 20260603204448.png]]

1st band represents the tens place
2nd band represents the ones place
3rd band represents the multiplier
4th band represents the tolerance (margin of error)

The resistor in the image above has a resistance of 4700$\ohm$ or 4.7k$\ohm$, with a tolerance of $\pm$ 10%.

1st band (tens digit) :  YELLOW (4)
2nd band (ones digit) : PURPLE (7)
3rd band (multiplier) : RED (100)
4th band (tolerance) : SILVER (10%)
#### 5 Colored Band Resistors:

![[Pasted image 20260603205548.png]]

1st band represents the hundreds place
2nd band represents the tens place
3rd band represents the ones place
4th band represents the multiplier
5th band represents the tolerance (margin of error)

The resistor in the image above has a resistance of 68000$\ohm$ or 68k$\ohm$, with a tolerance of $\pm$ 5%.
