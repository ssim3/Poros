- A,B,C represent the 3 vertices of a triangle
- $\theta$ is the angle at vertex C. Angles can be measured in degrees / radians

- opp = AB, the length opposite the angle theta
- adjacent = BC, the length **adjacent** to angle theta
- hypotenuse = AC, this is the triangles longest side.

AB = the height of the triangle, h.

# Trigonometric Ratios

SOH -> sin = opp / hypotenuse
CAH -> cosine = adj / hypotenuse
TOA -> tangent = opp / adj
